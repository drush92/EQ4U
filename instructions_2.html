But where do these words come from?

Those who work in the audio industry (e.g. music producers, acousticians, sound engineers) often use descriptive words to convey the quality or sonic character of a sound. Ordinarily, people use words such as ‘harsh’ or ‘bassy’ that are sometimes more subjective. However, there are descriptors used amongst audio professionals that are more or less standardised.

‘Warm’
Warm isn’t a very specific descriptor, but a warm sound is always characterised by an abundance of lower frequencies and/or a lack of higher frequencies.
